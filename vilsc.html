Vancouver Island Library Staff Conference 2026

Friday, May 8, 2026
University of Victoria
9:00 - 10:30 AM
Keynote: Vickery Bowles - Lam Auditorium
Vickery Bowles (Toronto's City Librarian 2015-2025) discusses the vital role of public libraries in supporting social connections, civic engagement, and democracy.
SESSION 1 (10:45 - 11:30 AM)
1A: Trans(formation): Digitizing the Rikki Swin Institute Collection - D101
Review of a project to digitize 519 audio-visual items documenting Trans+ activism (1970s-2000s), focusing on ethical tensions and community-created best practices. Presenter: Michael Radmacher (UVic Libraries)
1B-1: Relational Leadership and Relational Coordination - D114
Introduction to relational coordination as a cycle of continuous improvement to streamline library processes and recognize collective learning. Presenter: Melissa J. Goodchild (GVPL)
1B-2: Strategies for Introverts and the Socially Anxious - D114
Exploration of inclusive classroom techniques that move beyond verbal participation to support introverted or socially anxious students. Presenters: Tina Bebbington & Bill Blair (UVic Libraries)
1C-1: Beginning the Journey: UVic's Indigenous Hub - D115
A look at the creation of a digital space that surfaces local Indigenous collections and shares reconciliation actions through web design and curation. Presenters: Caitlin Keenan, Jessie Lampreau, & Artie Goshulak (UVic Libraries)
1C-2: Vibe Coding Language Revitalization Tools - D115
How to use generative AI to build custom web applications, like language soundboards, without traditional programming knowledge. Presenter: Rich McCue (UVic Libraries)
1D: Censorship in Western Canadian School Libraries - D103
Discussion on the landscape of Information Freedom, far-right censorship tactics, and recent challenges in the south island area. Presenter: Adam Reese (GVPL)
1E: It's Like Talking to a Good Friend (AI) - D110
Discussion on the irreplaceability of library workers as generative AI increasingly acts as a counselor and content creator. Presenters: Allison Jones & Brooklyn Kemp (GVPL)
SESSION 2 (11:45 AM - 12:30 PM)
2A: Everything Old is LLM Again (AI) - D101
How digitized historical collections are used to build better AI systems by allowing models to encounter historical uncertainty. Presenter: Corey Davis (UVic Libraries)
2B: Evolution of Service Points in McPherson Library - D115
A 60-year retrospective on how library use and design theory have reshaped physical circulation and reference spaces. Presenter: Page DeWolfe (UVic Libraries)
2C: What Is...Data Literacy? (Jeopardy-Style) - D114
An interactive session covering terminology, visualization, security, and accessibility through a Jeopardy-style game. Presenter: Cairo Sanders (UVic Libraries)
2D: BC's Historic Traveling Libraries - D103
Exploration of the Provincial Library's Traveling Libraries program (begun 1898) and how historical outreach models inform today. Presenters: Ciara O'Kelly & Anna Loster (Legislative Library of BC)
2E-1: Reversing Outreach (Lightning Talk) - D110
A strategy to reach out to the research community to learn about their work rather than simply "pushing" library services. Presenter: Krista Godfrey (UVic Libraries)
2E-2: Ten Years of KULA D110
Reflecting on a decade of the scholarly journal KULA and its exploration of knowledge production and technology. Presenters: Karina Greenwood, Samantha MacFarlane, & Caroline Winter (UVic Libraries)
SESSION 3 (1:30 - 2:15 PM)
3B: Permacomputing Community of Practice
Workshop on sustainable computing, maximizing hardware lifespan, and reducing reliance on extractive proprietary systems. Presenters: John Durno (UVic), Dana McFarland & Luke McLeod (VIU)
3C: Identification, Identity, and Community Participation
Logistics and impact of ID Clinics and TransID Clinics offered through partnerships with community organizations. Presenters: Megan Clark, Nina Mosallaei, & Caitlin Ottenbreit (GVPL)
SESSION 4 (2:30 - 3:15 PM)
4B: Information Literacy in Legal Research
Transitioning legal research from print to generative AI through interactive tutorial modules and ethical engagement. Presenters: Sarah Miller, Emily Nickerson, & Keilah Abello (UVic Libraries)
4D: Recommending Nonfiction to Adult Readers
Tips for pairing fiction and narrative nonfiction (biographies, true crime, travel) in reader's advisory. Presenter: Nicholle Akin (GVPL)
